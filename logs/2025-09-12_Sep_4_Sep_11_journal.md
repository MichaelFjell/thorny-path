- Set a timer for 10 minutes.  
- At the start of each minute, do 2–3 pull-ups (clean form, 1–2 reps in reserve).  
- The rest of the minute = rest.  
- When the next minute starts, do it again.  
- After 10 minutes, you’ve done 10 sets → ~20–30 total reps, spread out, without grinding to failure.  

Why it works: it builds volume and frequency, grooving the movement pattern, without frying your CNS like 3×to-failure would. Perfect for your “pull-up weak link” phase.
